A potted history

My original training was in performance. My history and work is all connected by narrative, and a sense of curiousity and wonder.

In 1995 I wrote my theatre Master's thesis on the possibilities technology offered to performance, in a philosophical and practical sense. I taught myself 3D modeling and animation to make a virtual performance space.

I went on to make early digital art seen internationally via Cornell University.
I worked as a 3D modeller on official Blur and Oasis themed Interactive Songbooks, and did some world building for Mirage Studios.
I taught interactive multimedia to troubled kids in failing schools.

All this time I was teaching Physical Theatre in an acting school.
Two worlds.
Following my heart, not the money.

One day I was asked to teach animation with this new software: Flash™. And it all came together. Live interaction combined with animation, driven by a story.

I moved to London as a Flash Developer. Mostly dry work. I got bored and left to work with independant artists on gallery installations, dance works and finally started teaching in the drama department at Royal Holloway University, running my course Technology Unplugged.

But my heart couldn't pay the bills.

I contracted, eventually falling in with the RKCR:y&r offspring agency Saint. We worked on great projects. Made some games, broke the internet with digg and had fun until the Flash™ bubble burst.

As a last hurrah I wrote a custom framework in ActionScript 3 for Jaguar Land Rover press iPad apps.
(Can't run on iOS, too slow?! Bah! Mumble grumble....)

Now, I occasionally play at making games, explore animation through particle systems, and ... whittle wood.

Sometimes it's just nice to reflect.

Historical Portfolio

iPad Apps

2007 - 2008

I was working for an agency who had Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) and Bentley as clients, making print press releases. They wanted to give all reviewing journalists iPads with all the images, data, video... but they couldn't afford a iOS developer.

I was convinced I could make what they needed with ActionScript. One app, updatable per press release.

The agency wasn't convinced, so I made a prototype with a photographer friend, showcasing his high quality images of Concrete Wonders.

It worked! Full screen swipable images and video. A 3D spinner. Masses of text, all internationalised with a 100% bespoke solution.
Super proud of that.
